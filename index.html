Mutuum Finance: A Comprehensive Guide to the Decentralized Lending Protocol

Introduction to Mutuum Finance

Mutuum Finance is a decentralized lending protocol built on Ethereum (with future multi-chain expansion) that revolutionizes peer-to-peer (P2P) borrowing and lending. Unlike traditional DeFi lending platforms (like Aave or Compound), Mutuum introduces a non-liquidatable loan system, reducing risks for borrowers while maintaining capital efficiency for lenders.

Why Mutuum Finance Stands Out?

✔ No forced liquidations – Borrowers keep assets even if collateral value drops.
✔ Algorithmic interest rates – Dynamic pricing based on supply/demand.
✔ DAO governance – Community-driven decision-making.
✔ Permissionless & transparent – Fully on-chain, no intermediaries.

2. Core Philosophy & Mission

Mutuum Finance aims to:

Democratize lending by eliminating bank-like restrictions.
Protect borrowers from predatory liquidations.
Maximize capital efficiency for lenders with competitive yields.
Integrate with real-world finance (RWA lending in the future).

3. How Mutuum Finance Works

3.1 Borrowing Mechanism
Users deposit collateral (e.g., ETH, BTC, stablecoins) into smart contracts.
Borrowers receive stablecoin loans (USDC, DAI) up to a customizable Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio.
Unlike Aave/Compound, loans are not liquidated if collateral value drops. Instead, repayment terms adjust.

3.2 Lending Mechanism
Lenders deposit funds into liquidity pools to earn interest.
Interest rates are algorithmically adjusted based on utilization rates.
Lenders can exit pools anytime (no lock-up periods).

3.3 Collateralization & Risk Management
Overcollateralization required (e.g., 150% LTV).
If collateral value drops below LTV threshold:
Grace period for repayment.
If unpaid, collateral is gradually converted to stablecoins to cover debt (no sudden liquidations).
